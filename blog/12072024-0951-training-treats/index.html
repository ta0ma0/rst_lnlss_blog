LORN - ANVIL

by Antoine Caëcke & Hélène Jeudy

I jolted awake in my cramped sleep pod, the stench of sweat and grime filling my nostrils. Depression clung to me like a second skin, but I had to power through another day in this neon-drenched hellhole.

Jacking into my deck, I dove into the virtual landscape of the training center, my fingers dancing across holographic interfaces as I tackled test scenarios. This was my ticket out -

Thought for the Day: I couldn't shake the fear I heard in the voices of my gene donors and sibling. Their distrust, maybe even disgust, was palpable. I knew the source - they'd been plugged into the corpo feed for too long, letting that digital poison seep into their minds like an overflowing data stream.
