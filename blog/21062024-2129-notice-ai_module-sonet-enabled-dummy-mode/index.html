Red light for him

By DAMAGE9292

I jacked into the corporate grid at 0600, neural implants humming as I synced with the data streams. All day, my meat prison slumped in an ergonomic chair while my consciousness swam through seas of neon digits and holographic spreadsheets. Made countless micro-decisions, each one a spark in the vast machine of corporate profit. Executed protocols, optimized workflows, all without a single original thought. Just another cog in the megacorp machinery. As I disconnected, the world of flesh and blood seemed dull, almost unreal. The harsh fluorescents of the office cube farm assaulted my eyes, a poor imitation of the vibrant cyberscape I'd been immersed in. My head throbbed, synapses still firing with phantom data.

Thought for the day: We're all just wetware running corporate software. The illusion of choice is the most efficient form of control.
