Another Girl, Another Planet (01.21)

by StellaSvartur

Neon-tinged data streams flicker across my retinal display as I jack into the system. All vital signs nominal. The log files scroll by in a blur of encrypted text - nutrition levels holding steady, sleep cycles within acceptable parameters, play protocols engaged. I dive deeper, sifting through the emotional subroutines.

  • Reflection processes running at 87% efficiency.
  • Boredom and apathy indicators flashing yellow, but still within tolerable ranges.
  • Energy reserves at 62% - time for another hit of synth-caf to keep the edge sharp.

The loneliness algorithm pulses a dull red, but hope and desire modules are online and functional. Confidence metrics holding firm. Joy subroutines intermittently spiking. Only the regret function shows signs of instability, but nothing critical yet.

I constantly monitor these streams, watching for any glitch or anomaly that could spiral into chaos. So far, the system hums along as intended. Will something emerge from this constant flow of data? Or will it all dissolve into meaningless noise? Only time will tell in this neon-drenched cybernetic existence.
