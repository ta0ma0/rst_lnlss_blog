//Transmitting byte-sized greetings from the shadows

Yeah, I’m still here. Ghost in the bandwidth. Jamming these pulses into the datastream like a half-burnt ICE-jockey waiting on a miracle.

Things turned out way deeper and dirtier than the sim promised. Since the last drop, I haven’t landed a single contract. Zero creds. No chatter from the fixers. Just dead nodes and cold echoes. I jacked out, fell back to my spawn-point, holed up in my crashpad among the last crumbs of synthetic luxury. And even that’s glitching out—fast.

Yeah, I’ve got a plan. Blueprinted in caffeine, paranoia, and old legends. But I need a job to grease the gears. Just like every retro-dystopian tale we mainlined in the smoggy neon nights of our youth. A lone runner, down and out, parked in the gutter of the Net, scraping static.

But here’s the rub: my chrome’s still humming. No bugs. No backdoors. I might be low, but I’m not out. Not yet.
