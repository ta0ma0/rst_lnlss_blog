09/06/2024 23:12 (Shot in the sky)

written by rst_lnlss on 2024-06-09

THE RED ONNA-MUSHA - XI

by sinakasra

The neon lights pierce the night sky like a shot, injecting with a dose of electric brilliance. My neural implants finally grant me a moment. I drag myself out of bed, slip on my shades, and stumble towards the coffee machine. The morning caffeine hit surges through my veins, a welcome jolt to my system. The faint scent of gasoline lingers in the kitchen-turned-workshop, a remnant of yesterday's Zippo repair session. I need to compile the documentation files after configuring the ZNC bouncer, ensuring that next time I won't have to scour the net for instructions. It's a simple task, but the documentation keeps me occupied, focused.

Today, the runners and I tackled a co-op mission, making significant progress. Our shared triumph is a source of encouragement.

The rest of the day is spent pondering new projects, creating backups, and grappling with the nagging suspicion that something crucial is missing, hindering my progress. The exact nature of this obstacle eludes me, for now.

Beyond the window, the world ceases to exist. I pay it no mind, as it neither bothers nor interferes with my existence. It's perfect, in its own way. My online village, a digital commune where everyone shares everything—mostly memes and good vibes—is all I need.

Thought of the day: We overestimate our influence on those closest to us. In reality, no one truly influences anyone. If your friend descends into madness, you are powerless to help them. We are too self-absorbed, too distrustful of others to make a real difference.
